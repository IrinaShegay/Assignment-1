5. Quokka.


The quokka, also known as the short-tailed scrub wallaby , is a small macropod about the size of a domestic cat. It is the only member of the genus Setonix. Like other marsupials in the macropod family, the quokka is herbivorous and mainly nocturnal. Quokkas are found on some smaller islands off the coast of Western Australia, particularly Rottnest Island just off Perth and Bald Island near Albany. Isolated, scattered populations also exist in forest and coastal heath between Perth and Albany.


4. Hedgehog.


A hedgehog is a spiny mammal of the subfamily Erinaceinae, in the eulipotyphlan family Erinaceidae. Like many of the first mammals, they have adapted to a nocturnal way of life. Their spiny protection resembles that of porcupines, which are rodents, and echidnas, a type of monotreme.


3. Black-Footed Cat.


The black-footed cat is a mammal from the feline family, one of the smallest modern representatives of this family. It is found in the desert areas of southern Africa. Color - sandy yellow with dark spots. black-footed cats occupy rabbit burrows or termite mounds. In 2002, the black-footed cat was given the status of a vulnerable species. In 1962, Paul Leyhausen managed to cross a black-footed cat with a domestic cat.


2. Red Panda.


The red panda is a carnivoran native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. The red panda has reddish-brown fur, a long, shaggy tail, and a waddling gait due to its shorter front legs; it is roughly the size of a domestic cat, though with a longer body. It is arboreal and feeds mainly on bamboo, but also eats eggs, birds, and insects. It is a solitary animal, mainly active from dusk to dawn, and is largely sedentary during the day. It is also called the lesser panda, the red bear-cat , and the fire fox .Island near Albany. Isolated, scattered populations also exist in forest and coastal heath between Perth and Albany.


1. Fennec Fox.


The fennec fox is a small crepuscular fox native to the Sahara Desert, the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt, and the Arava and Negev deserts in Israel. Its most distinctive feature is its unusually large ears, which serve to dissipate heat. The fennec is the smallest canid species. Its coat, ears, and kidney functions have adapted to the desert environment with high temperatures and little water.
